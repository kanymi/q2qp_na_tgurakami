Initiatives 先手

2024 Tokyo International Innovation Awards

The OST founded the TIIA in 2005, and has since to be spread all across the globe. Many innovators compete for the ultimate prize of achieving the Tokyo International Innovation Award. This year, the TIIA will be on September 2024. Click the button below to learn more.

Campaign for Bionics: Helping the Disabled have access to Prosthetics

Bionics are a branch of technology that deals with robotic limbs, otherwise known as prosthetics. Starting in 2012, the OST has been committed to providing inexpensive yet next=generation bionics for the disabled.

Project Titanium: Reinforcing Japan's buildings against Disasters

Earthquakes are very common in Japan. That is why we have created Project Titanium, a new concept designed to stabilize buildings, especially Skyscrapers in Japan. This is to help prevent injury of citizents, or even loss of life.

Involvements 関与

Achieving New Heights in Robotics: OST collaborates with KIT

The Odaiba Insitute of Sci & Tech and the Kyushu Institution of Technology (Kyutech) have made an ease of access to Robotic Engineering by providing starter kits to individuals under 8 y.o. who are planning to pursue STEM

The 2011 9.1 Magnitude Japan Earthquake: 13 Years Later

Helping citizens with restoration of buildings by providing supplies and financial aid. We helped the governments of Miyagi, Fukushima, Chiba, and Ibaraki in their own restoration projects.

Donating Microscopes to Schools around Japan: Improving Education in the Nation

The OST and Japanese Science and Technology Agency have collectively donated over 1000 microscopes to underdeveloped schools in Japan.

Saving the Planet from Climate Change: Plants Preventing Pollution

By helping against climate change, the OST and the JSTA have made several advances and studies in the field of Biology and Agriculture to provide the optimal technique for prevention.
